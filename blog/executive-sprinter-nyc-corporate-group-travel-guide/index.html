Booking Best Practices for Corporate Accounts

Advance Planning

  • Routine needs: Book 48-72 hours ahead
  • High-demand periods: Book 1-2 weeks ahead (Fashion Week, UN General Assembly, major conferences)
  • Holiday travel: Book as early as possible

Communication Essentials

When booking, provide:

  • Exact passenger count (confirm day-before)
  • Luggage requirements
  • All stop addresses with contact names
  • Any special requirements (WiFi critical, ADA needs, etc.)
  • Billing information (corporate account, PO number)

Day-Of Coordination

  • Designate one group contact for driver communication
  • Share the driver's direct number with that contact
  • Have a backup plan discussed (though rarely needed)
  • Confirm all passengers have the pickup location

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Underestimating Time

NYC traffic is unpredictable. Build buffer time into every leg of a multi-stop day. Better to arrive early and circle than rush between meetings.

Ignoring Luggage Requirements

Even Sprinters have limits. If every passenger has a roller bag plus laptop bag, make sure the provider knows. Nothing worse than not fitting everyone's luggage.

Vague Scheduling

"Flexible pickup around 2 PM" creates problems. Drivers are assigned to specific times. Be as precise as possible, then adjust if needed.

Not Establishing a Single Point of Contact

Eight people texting the driver creates confusion. One person coordinates, everyone else follows that lead.

Forgetting the Return

Book outbound and return at the same time when possible. Last-minute Sprinter availability isn't guaranteed.
